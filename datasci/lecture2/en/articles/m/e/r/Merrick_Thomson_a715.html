Merrick Thomson (born September 9, 1982) is a lacrosse player from Hamilton, Ontario, Canada. He plays the attack position for the New Jersey Pride of Major League Lacrosse[1], and for the Philadelphia Wings of the National Lacrosse League.

[edit] College career

Thomson played collegiate lacrosse at the University at Albany. He was co-captain of the Great Danes, alongside Frank Resetarits, and helped lead his team to the NCAA Men's Lacrosse Championship tournament as a senior. [2] Thomson made an immediate impact in the NCAA. As a freshman, he equaled the NCAA single game record of playoff goals scored, with six, in a contest against the Syracuse Orangemen, earning America East Conference Rookie of the Year in 2004.[1] In 2005, he led the nation in goals scored per game, with 3.38 per game, and in 2007 was named an All-American (third-team). [3]

[edit] Professional career

Thomson plays in Major League Lacrosse with the New Jersey Pride. In limited playing time, Thomson impressed, and has proved himself a valuable asset in the future to the Pride. [4]

Thomson was selected in the first round (second overall) by the Philadelphia Wings of the National Lacrosse League in the 2007 NLL Entry Draft. A consensus first round draft pick, the Wings chose Thomson to strengthen their left handed offensive attack. [5] Thomson captured Rookie of the Week honors in week 4 of the 2008 NLL season, and also was named Rookie of the Month for January. [6]

[edit] Awards

  • 2004 – America East Conference Rookie of the Year [3]
